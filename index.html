About The center

The National Council for Training is the government institution responsible for developing professional performance of Government employees through training and continuing education. Since its initiation in 1949, the National Council for Training has undergone several changes in its organizational affiliation, currently the NCT functions under the Ministry of Labor and Social Development.

The NCT is responsible for:
  • Setting training policies and regulations in accordance with the capacity building strategy of the Government entities, while taking into consideration the unique requirements for each organization.
  • Assist Government units in bridging skills gap through organizing and implementing national and international capacity building training courses (six month or less) and post graduate degrees (more than six months).

Goals and Strategy

Our Goals

The National Training Council conduct its specific tasks in accordance with the state directives that supports human resources development training and capacity building in all levels to achieve comprehensive and sustainable development. Taking this in consideration, the NCT developed the following specific goals:

  • Implementation of Systemic training for all Government employees
  • Providing training based on measurable learning objectives
  • Applying Training Quality Standards (ISO 10015 Training)
  • Provision of equal training opportunities for all Government employees
Our Strategy
  • Enhance the performance of Government employees through implementing robust and innovative training programs that will raise their knowledge, skills, and job specific requirements.
  • Enhancing the quality of training and capacity building for professionals, skilled workers, and technicians in all Government sectors.
  • Develop and transform NCT to a proactive and innovative organization.
  • Mobilize resources to support NCT

NCT Historical Background

  • The National Council for training was established in 1949 as a Scholarship Division within the Bureau of Civil Service
  • In 1967 the division expanded and became The Secretariat for National Committee for Scholarships.
  • During the period 1969-1971 it was affiliated to the Ministry of Planning.
  • In 1982 it became an independent unit affiliated to the Council of Ministers and was renamed the National Directorate for Training.
  • In 1993 the National Directorate for Training was affiliated to the Ministry of Manpower (formerly the Ministry of Labor and Administrative Reform), and name was changed to General Directorate for Human Resource Development
  • In accordance with the National Training Law 2003, it became the General Directorate for the National Council for Training.
  • In 2019, and under the newly established Transitional Government, it was affiliated to the Ministry of Labor and Social Development.
